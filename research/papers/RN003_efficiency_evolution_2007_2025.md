## NSE Market Efficiency Evolution: 2007-2025

**Status:** Confirmed
**Date:** May 2026

---

## Question

Is the mean reversion effect weakening as NSE develops and matures?
If yes — the trading edge has a shelf life and urgency matters.
If no — the edge is structural and durable.

---

## Method

Computed mean lag-1 autocorrelation across all NSE stocks
for each calendar year 2007-2025.
Fitted a linear time trend to test for systematic change.

## Trend Analysis

| Test | Result |

| Trend slope | +0.0007/year |
| P-value | 0.76 |
| R-squared | 0.006 |
| Significant | NO |

Excluding outliers (2008 crisis, 2013 bull):
slope = -0.001/year, p = 0.36 — still not significant,
direction reverses to slight strengthening.

---

## Notable Observations

**2008 — strongest effect (-0.21, 95.7% negative)**
Post-election crisis and GFC created extreme dislocations.
Maximum chaos produces maximum mean reversion signal.

**2013 — only positive year (+0.04)**
NSE's strongest bull market year. Strong trending markets
temporarily suppress cross-sectional mean reversion.

**2022-2024 — strengthening (-0.11, -0.09, -0.09)**
Recent years show the effect getting stronger, not weaker.

**2025 — near zero**
Current strong bull market (+51% YTD) suppressing effect,
consistent with 2013 observation.

---

## Conclusion

NSE mean reversion is statistically stable over 18 years.
No meaningful evidence of efficiency improvement.
The time trend is indistinguishable from zero (p=0.76).

The effect is structural — reflecting the fundamental
microstructure of a frontier market with limited participants,
low liquidity, and slow price discovery.
These conditions are not changing materially.

**The trading edge identified in RN002 is durable.**
No urgency to exploit before it disappears.
Build the strategy correctly.
